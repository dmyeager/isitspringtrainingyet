Volume I · No. 7 · Wednesday, the Fifteenth of July, Two Thousand and Twenty-Six

Reporting 1 contest from the day prior.

The Game of the Day

THE JUNIOR CIRCUIT PUTS THE SENIOR TO THE SWORD, FOUR TO NAUGHT

In the Midsummer Classic Beneath the Philadelphia Lamps, the American League's Paladins Blank Their Rivals and Anoint Bellinger Their Champion

The grand exhibition, so long trumpeted, proved no idle pageant but a genuine throttling. Before a throng of three-and-forty thousand nine hundred and sixteen gathered at Citizens Bank Park, the American League's chosen fell upon the senior circuit's finest in the very first frame and never once loosened their grip, prevailing four runs to none. The host town's own Cristopher Sánchez of the Phils toed the slab for the National League and found the footing treacherous: with two men aboard, the Yankees' Cody Bellinger lashed a two-run single to center, and home trotted Yordan Alvarez and Shea Langeliers; hard upon it Ben Rice singled home Bobby Witt Jr., and the tally stood three-to-naught before the senior league had scarcely drawn breath.

From there the National bats were but a rumor — three hits all told, no run to show, and five-and-ten times set down on strikes by a relentless procession of junior-loop arms. In the eighth, the White Sox's Miguel Vargas, come lately into the fray at the third station, launched a solitary blast to left for the fourth and final marker. Toronto's Dylan Cease earned the decision at one-and-naught with a spotless inning and three men fanned, while Sánchez wore the loss. And when the smoke had cleared it was Bellinger — two runs driven home upon his lone base knock — whom the assembled scribes hailed Most Valuable, a Bronx man crowned prince of the summer's one true all-star night. No pennant was moved nor standing shifted; yet bragging rights, that most imperishable of coin, flew home this morning with the American League.

News Around the League

The Wire Hums Yet Through the Recess

Though the diamonds lay dark and the scoreboards dim, the telegraph key would not consent to rest. The Brew Crew of Milwaukee shipped right-hander Easton McGee to Kansas City in exchange for hard coin, while Detroit dealt outfielder Jahmai Jones to Boston for a player to be named in some later dispatch. Small barter, perhaps, in the grand ledger of the season — but proof that even in the lull the clubhouses keep their counsel and their commerce.

The Sick List and the Sundry Shuffle

Kansas City moved right-hander Nick Mears from the fifteen-day list to the sixty-day exile, a right shoulder impingement the stubborn culprit. Upon the mending road set out a small caravan of the convalescent — Minnesota's Cole Sands and Garrett Acton, together with Washington's Max Kranick and Trevor Williams, each dispatched to his club's Florida Complex nine, and the Dodgers' own Edwin Díaz to the Arizona Complex outfit. Elsewhere the roster-keepers tidied their ledgers against the resumption: the Metropolitans outrighted Dan Hammer to Syracuse, the North Siders sent Jake Woodford down to Iowa, the Nationals passed Matt Krook to Rochester, and the Hub nine of Boston optioned young Nate Eaton to Worcester.

The Intermission All But Spent

With the Midsummer Classic now written into the book, the great pause draws to its close, and the two circuits shall shortly take up the true hostilities once more. The half-season ledger — the Boys in Blue of Los Angeles astride all creation, the pennant races of both loops drawn tight — awaits its second telling, and every faithful reader is bidden to keep his wireless warm, for the flags shall furl no longer and the real reckoning resumes within days.

And so the summer's one manufactured quarrel is settled, the American League's paladins having blanked their senior rivals four-to-naught beneath the Philadelphia lamps, with the Bronx man Bellinger bearing off the laurels. It counts for nothing in the pennant arithmetic and for everything in the taproom argument — which is, after all, the whole delicious point of the thing. Rest ye well this one last morning of the recess, for the true campaign resumes anon and the scorebooks hunger to be filled. Until the morrow's dispatch, keep your scorecards near and your enthusiasms nearer.
