Volume I · No. 8 · Thursday, the Sixteenth of July, Two Thousand and Twenty-Six

No contests this day; the hot stove burns bright.

0 days until Second-Half Play Resumes (Thursday, the Sixteenth of July).

News Around the League

The Long Recess Ends This Very Eve

Rejoice, ye faithful, for the intermission is spent and the National Pastime stirs once more. This very evening, at ten minutes past seven o'clock by Eastern reckoning, a single lamp shall be lit beneath the Citizens Bank arc-lights, where the Flushing nine of New York come calling upon the host Philadelphias — the selfsame yard that but two nights past played stage to the summer's grand exhibition. It is a modest reopening, one lone contest to break the silence; the full sixteen-fold card does not unfurl until the morrow, Friday the Seventeenth.

Yet what a tableau this opener presents. The men of Philadelphia return to the fray at four-and-fifty against three-and-forty, a bare two lengths adrift of the Dixie nine of Atlanta atop the senior East and holding fast to a wild-card berth of their own. Their guests from Flushing come in altogether humbler estate — two-score against seven-and-fifty, buried a full sixteen games from the summit and playing now for pride and the autumn's draft position. The Herald bids every reader draw near his wireless, for after five dark days the true hostilities resume where the pageant left off, hard by the Delaware.

The Battle Lines, As the Clubs Take the Field Anew

The half-year ledger stands closed, and it is the Angelenos of Los Angeles who bestride the whole of creation — one-and-sixty against six-and-thirty, a mark no club in either loop can touch. In the senior circuit's heartland the Suds men of Milwaukee reign at nine-and-fifty against seven-and-thirty, five clear of the Bruins of Chicago, while in the East the Dixie nine of Atlanta hold a slender pair of games over those same Philadelphias.

Across the junior loop the Gulf Coast club of Tampa Bay top the East, three lengths ahead of the Bronx Bombers of New York — whose scarred wounded aside, the pinstriped men own the finest run-mark in all the American League and a commanding grip upon the wild card. The Central remains a genuine deadlock, the South Siders of Chicago and the Forest City nine of Cleveland knotted to the very hundredth at .526; the West belongs, for now, to the Arlington men of Texas by a game and a half. Mark well, too, the Fenway men of Boston, who sprint into the second half upon a nine-game fire and scratch loudly at the postseason door, while the Athletics limp in having stumbled the opposite direction. And should any doubt the arms that shall decide it all, let him note that Milwaukee's Jacob Misiorowski carried a positively miserly one-and-sixty-two-hundredths earned-run mark into the recess, and Houston's Yordan Alvarez a firm double crown of one-and-thirty home runs and three-score-and-ten runs driven in.

Doings at the Wire Through the Recess

Even with the diamonds dark, the telegraph key would not lie still. The South Siders of Chicago shipped left-hander Brandon Eisert and infielder Jacob Gonzalez to the Pittsburgh Buccaneers in exchange for young Jaden Woods and future considerations, while the Suds men of Milwaukee purchased infielder Braden Shewmake from the Houston nine and added right-hander Bryse Wilson off the free-agent heap besides. The Kansas City nine, for their part, inked the well-traveled Vince Velasquez to a fresh contract.

And a crop of veterans found themselves set adrift as the clubs tidied their books against the resumption: the Bruins of Chicago parted with right-hander Kyle Wright, the Philadelphias released outfielder Dylan Carlson, the Arlington men cut loose Jonah Bride, and the Redbirds of St. Louis waved farewell to Bligh Madris. Small barter in the season's grand ledger, perhaps — but proof that even in the lull no clubhouse keeps entirely idle hands.

And so the great pause is ended, the scorebooks that lay open these five days at the half-year mark to be inscribed afresh come nightfall. We note the Angelenos astride all creation, the Suds men and the Dixie nine and the Gulf Coast club holding their several thrones, the junior Central still deadlocked to the hundredth, and the Fenway men carrying a nine-game blaze back into the fray. One lone contest lights the lamps this evening by the Delaware, the Flushing nine upon the Philadelphias, ere the full card returns in its sixteen-fold glory tomorrow. Sharpen your pencils, for the true reckoning is upon us once more. Until the next dispatch, keep your scorecards near and your enthusiasms nearer.
